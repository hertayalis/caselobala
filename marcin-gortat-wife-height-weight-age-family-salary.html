Biography (Age)

Marcin Gortat was born on February 17, 1984 in Lodz, Poland where he was raised by his athlete parents; Janusz and Alicia Gortat. Under the influence of his parents, Gortat as a child actively participated in sports. specifically football and athletics. He then turned to basketball and decided to focus on the more popular and lucrative game across the Atlantic Ocean.

After graduating from a technical school in college, Gortat joined his local basketball team, LKS Lodz, where his performances attracted the interest of German professional basketball team RheinEnergie Cologne in 2003. After two years with his new team, Marcin Gortat was selected by the Phoenix Suns in the second round of the 2005 NBA season with the 57th pick. However, the player chose to stay in Germany for another year to continue his development. The following season, his team won the German national championship and, for the first time in its history, played EuroLeague basketball in the 2006-07 season.

In August 2007, Gortat joined the NBA and joined the Orlando Magic who had acquired his trade rights from the team that drafted him. Gortat spent some time with Anaheim Arsenal in the NBA Development League before he could make his debut on March 1, 2008, playing just 6 games that season, averaging 3.0 points and 2.7 rebounds per game. The player didn’t enjoy much playing time as he was used as a substitute for Dwight Howard. However, he was lucky enough to boast that he was the only Polish player to have played in the NBA Finals when the team got there. 2009.

In December 2010, Marcin Gortat was traded to the Phoenix Suns with whom he enjoyed very well. Previously averaging around 12 minutes per game, Gortat was now an integral part of the Suns squad and was averaging around 30 minutes per game. His time with the Suns, however, lasted three seasons before being traded to the Washington Wizards. From 2013 to 2018 Gortat, who now went by the moniker ‘Polish Hammer’, came very close to a double-double despite actually pulling off the feat in 2016-17.

Marcin Gortat Family
