Aljamain Sterling: Biography

Mixed martial artist Aljamain Sterling, who is of Jamaican and American descent, has two brothers by the names of Troy Sterling and Kevin Sterling. The three brothers have dedicated their lives to the field of mixed martial arts.

Aljamain currently holds the title of UFC Bantamweight Champion and competes in the Bantamweight division of the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC).

Additionally, he competed in the Cage Fury Fighting Championship, in which he had previously competed and won the bantamweight title.

As of the 23rd of August in 2022, he holds the seventh spot in the pound-for-pound rankings for men in the UFC.

In 2009, Sterling won his first fight as an amateur by way of submission. Prior to turning professional, he had a record of 6-1 as an amateur, with his lone defeat coming by way of a split decision.

He would go on to avenge that defeat later in his career. During the course of his career, Sterling was able to win the amateur featherweight championship for Extreme FC as well as the amateur bantamweight championship for Raging Wolf.

    Aljamain Sterling: Career Record

    Sterling competed for the first time in the Ultimate Fighting Championship in the bout against Cody Gibson. In the end, the judgment was reached unanimously in his favor.

    It is common knowledge that the man known as the “Funk Master” was only a lightweight of average skill. He faced Marlon Moraes in a bout that was important for his career, but he was knocked out by Moraes in just 67 seconds, which was a devastating setback for him.

    After suffering this defeat, he proceeded to win his next four fights, including the title eliminator against Cory Sandhagen that took place at UFC 250.

    “Funk Master” finished the battle in less than 88 seconds by submitting his opponent in the first round (RNC). His dominance over the other competitors earned him the title of the evening’s most outstanding performer, and he was the first boxer ever to receive this honor.

    He was defeated by Petr Yan in the bout for the bantamweight belt. The ‘Funk Master,’ a fighter who had already competed 15 times in the UFC and had a record of 12-3, was able to win the belt after the previous champion was disqualified for breaking the rules.

    He was the first UFC fighter to win the belt by disqualification, which cemented his place as a pioneer in the sport.
