Mary Kay Place Biography

Mary Kay Place is an American actress, singer, director, and screenwriter who was born and brought up in Tulsa, Oklahoma, United States of America. She is widely known for her role as Loretta Haggers on the television series Mary Hartman, a role that won her the year 1977 Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress – Comedy Series.

Her numerous film appearances include Private Benjamin (1980), The Big Chill (1983), Captain Ron (1992) and Francis Ford Coppola’s 1997 drama The Rainmaker.

Mary also recorded three studio albums for Columbia Records, one in the Haggers persona, which included the Top Ten country music hit “Baby Boy.” For her performance in Diane, Place won the Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Actress as well as the National Society of Film Critics Award for Best Actress.
